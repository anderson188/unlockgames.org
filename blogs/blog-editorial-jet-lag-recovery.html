Jet Lag Recovery Without Supplements or Guesswork

2026-05-21 · Travel👁 19,340

Who this is for: Long-haul flyers crossing three or more time zones who want a plan beyond “stay awake until bedtime” and unproven pill stacks. Light and meal timing do boring, reliable work.

Jet lag is a schedule problem

Your body clock expects light, food, and sleep at familiar times.

Supplements promise shortcuts; circadian cues remain the lever you control.

Before you fly

Shift bedtime one hour toward destination for two nights if the trip is long.

If impossible, focus on arrival plan instead of partial shifts both ways.

On the plane

Eastbound: chase destination night—eye mask, skip alcohol, eat lightly.

Westbound: stay awake with destination day; walk aisles, hydrate.

First forty-eight hours on ground

Morning outdoor light anchors rhythm. Caffeine before local noon; avoid late espresso even if zombie.

Eat on local meal times even if appetite lags.

Naps with rules

Twenty minutes max before 3 p.m. local—or skip naps and accept one hard evening for faster sync.

Short trips vs long holidays

Under four days: sometimes partial adaptation wastes energy; run on home time for lightning business visits.

Long stays: commit to local time immediately including weekends.

What not to obsess over

Perfect sleep night one is rare. Functional day two matters more than eight hours in a unfamiliar bed.

FAQ

Melatonin? Optional; light and meals still do heavy lifting.

Red-eye eastbound? Hard but pair with morning light on landing.

Kids? Same cues; shorter wake windows need gentler transitions.

Key takeaways

  • Morning outdoor light on arrival
  • Local meal times even without hunger
  • Caffeine cutoff mid-afternoon local
  • Short trips may skip full adaptation
