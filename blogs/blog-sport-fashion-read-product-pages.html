How to Read Sportswear Product Pages (Fit, Fabrics, Care)

2026-03-28 · Sport & Fashion

Who this is for: Online shoppers who scroll photos but skip the spec block—and then wonder why the leggings arrived sheer or the jacket “runs small.”

Start with the spec block, not the headline

Marketing names (“CloudFeel™,” “IceDry”) are trademarks, not standards. Scroll to material percentages: e.g. 80% polyester / 20% elastane tells you stretch and sweat behavior better than adjectives. Natural fibers (cotton, wool) feel different from synthetics; blends aim to combine strengths.

Common fabrics and what they do

  • Polyester: Lightweight, quick-drying, durable; can retain odor without proper wash care.
  • Nylon: Smooth, strong, common in tights and shells; often used where abrasion resistance matters.
  • Elastane (Spandex/Lycra): Stretch and recovery; higher % usually means tighter compression.
  • Merino wool: Temperature regulation and odor resistance; often blended for sport layers.

Fit cues hiding in plain sight

Check model height, size worn, and inseam if listed—compare to your body. “Compression” vs “relaxed” or “oversized” are cut intents, not sizes. Read the size chart in your unit system (cm / inches) rather than guessing S/M/L across brands. Look for “long,” “short,” or “tall” lines if sleeves or inseams are often wrong for you.

Feature lists: what is worth weighing

Flatlock seams reduce chafe on long runs. Reflective hits matter if you train in low light. Zip pockets beat open pockets for keys on a run. Waterproof vs water-resistant: the former is heavier and less breathable; the latter is fine for drizzle only.

Care symbols and longevity

Heat destroys elastic fibers—avoid high dryer settings on leggings and sports bras. Fabric softener can clog moisture-wicking finishes. When a page says “wash inside out,” it is not optional if you want logos to last.

Returns and regional differences

International shoppers: confirm whether prices include VAT, who pays return shipping, and if final sale applies to markdowns. Some regions mandate longer return windows; others do not—read the footer policy, not the banner alone.

Common mistakes

Trusting one reviewer who is a different body type. Ignoring fabric weight (gsm) when comparing “same” hoodies. Buying final-sale race gear without trying the brand’s cut before.

Key takeaways

Use composition + size chart + model data + care as your four-step filter. Ignore empty superlatives until those check out.

FAQ

Is “sustainable” always meaningful? Look for specific certifications or recycled content %; vague green wording is weak signal.

Reviews say “runs small”—always? Treat as a hint; verify against the chart for your measurements.
